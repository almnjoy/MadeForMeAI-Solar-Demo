# TrueNorth Solar FAQ

Source: https://truenorth.madeformeai.com/faqs

## Is my home suitable for solar?
Depends on location, roof angle, shading, and age. TrueNorth advisors use advanced solar design technology to assess your specific home. Best way to find out is a free consultation.

## How many panels does my home need?
Depends on:
- Your annual kWh usage (from utility bills)
- Local climate / sunlight hours
- Roof orientation and available space
TrueNorth sizes the system during the free consultation using your 12-month usage history.

## Why is my electricity usage history important?
It lets TrueNorth design a system precisely sized to your consumption patterns, accounting for seasonal variation. Bring 12 months of bills to the consultation.

## Do solar panels work on cloudy days / in winter?
Yes — output is reduced on cloudy days and zero at night, but grid draw fills the gap. In winter, panels still work. Snow slides off the smooth glass surface once the sun warms the panel.

## Can I expand my system later?
Yes. TrueNorth can evaluate your existing system and energy needs to add more panels. Most systems last 25–30 years.

## What warranties are included?
- 25-year performance warranty on panels (manufacturer)
- Workmanship warranty on installation (TrueNorth)
- Other components carry their own manufacturer warranties

## What if my system isn't producing electricity?
Check the monitoring portal for alerts. Contact TrueNorth if something seems off — they'll help troubleshoot. TrueNorth does not monitor your system directly but provides you with a monitoring portal.

## How does solar affect my home insurance?
Notify your insurer. Your home's value may increase, so your coverage may need adjusting.

## What does TrueNorth Solar guarantee?
At least 85% of the estimated annual energy production outlined in the contract. If that target isn't met, TrueNorth will take corrective action.

## How does reimbursement work?
Contact TrueNorth to request it. May require documentation. Approval process takes several weeks; check delivery adds a few more weeks.
