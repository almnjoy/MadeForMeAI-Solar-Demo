# Solar 101 — How It Works

Source: https://truenorth.madeformeai.com/solar-101

## The Basic Process

1. Sunlight hits the solar cells inside the panels.
2. Photons excite electrons, creating direct current (DC) electricity.
3. An inverter converts DC to alternating current (AC) — the kind your home uses.
4. Electricity is used in the home, stored in a battery, or sent to the grid.

## Components of a Solar System

**Solar Panels**
Convert sunlight into electrical energy using the photovoltaic effect. Typically ~4ft x 6ft, ~30 lbs each. Grouped into an "array" on the roof.

**Inverters**
Convert DC electricity from panels into usable AC electricity. Three types:
- String inverters — one central unit, cost-effective, affected by shading
- Microinverters — one per panel, best performance, higher cost
- Power optimizers — hybrid approach, panel-level conditioning, central inverter

**Mounting / Racking Hardware**
Secures panels at optimal angle (30–45°, facing south). Includes rails, clamps, flashings.

**Monitoring Systems**
Tracks energy production and system health. Most are accessible via mobile app or online dashboard. Allows homeowners to spot performance drops early.

## Key Terms

**kW (Kilowatt)** — Measures the capacity of your system (how much it can produce at once).

**kWh (Kilowatt-hour)** — Measures energy produced or used over time. This is what shows up on your power bill.

**Photovoltaic effect** — The process by which sunlight excites electrons in solar cells to create electrical current.

## Common Questions

**Do panels work on cloudy days?**
Yes, but at reduced output. They produce no power at night, but grid draw covers that gap.

**What about winter / snow?**
Panels work in cold temps. Snow typically slides off due to the smooth glass surface and panel angle once the sun warms them.

**How long do panels last?**
Most systems last 25–30 years with minimal maintenance. Panels typically carry a 25-year performance warranty.
